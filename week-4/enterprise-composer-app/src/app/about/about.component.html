Duke Ellington


Duke Ellington was born Edward Kennedy Ellington on April 29, 1899, in Washington, D.C. He began playing piano at age 7 and studied art throughout high school. He spent his adult life as a jazz composer, pianist, and band leader after beginning his career at age 17. He was with his band for half a century and composed thousands of scores. He performed with the likes of Louis Armstrong, Ella Fitzgerald, and John Coltrane, and composed with Strayhorn very closely for years.
